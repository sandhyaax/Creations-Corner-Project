Welcome to the latest news section of Creations Corner. Here, we highlight innovative projects, share updates, and provide insights into fascinating concepts. Today, we dive into the fundamentals of Grashof's Law.

Grashof's Law is a principle in kinematics that determines whether a four-bar linkage will act as a crank, rocker, or double rocker. It states that for a four-bar linkage, the sum of the shortest and longest link lengths should be less than or equal to the sum of the remaining two link lengths for the linkage to have a full rotation of one link.
